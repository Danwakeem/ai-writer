## Sun bears in Hangzhou Zoo defended as 'real thing'
The Hangzhou Zoo has defended the authenticity of their sun bears after internet users questioned whether the animals were actually bears. The controversy arose after photos circulated showing one of the bears standing upright on its hind legs. Sun bears are smaller than other bear species and can stand at most 1.3 meters tall on their hind legs. Other Chinese zoos have faced similar accusations of passing off different animals as exotic species.

## Yellow Trucking Company Shuts Down and Files for Bankruptcy, Putting 30,000 Jobs at Risk
Yellow, one of the nation's largest less-than-truckload carriers, has ceased operations and filed for bankruptcy. The company's financial struggles, including a debt of $1.5 billion, have led to its closure, which puts 30,000 jobs at risk. If Yellow goes under, prices for shipments will increase as customers turn to other carriers. However, the freight market has enough capacity to handle the diversions and absorb the business.

## Michigan Supreme Court Rules in Favor of Injured Crash Victims' Lifetime Benefits
Michigan Supreme Court rules that a new state law cannot strip away ongoing benefits for crash victims. The law had set a fee schedule and cap on reimbursements not covered by Medicare, putting benefits at risk for approximately 18,000 people. The court deemed the benefits as a vested contractual right that cannot be diminished without declaring intent to do so.
