## Tesla CFO Kirkhorn steps down amid broader sell-off in electric vehicle sector
Tesla's chief financial officer, Zach Kirkhorn, has stepped down after four years in the position. The company gave no reason for his departure but highlighted its tremendous growth during his tenure. Vaibhav Taneja, the current chief accounting officer, has been named as the new CFO. Tesla's stock fell over 2% at the opening bell, along with other electric vehicle companies Rivian and Lucid.

## Tyson Foods closing four chicken processing plants to lower costs
Tyson Foods is closing four chicken processing plants located in North Little Rock, Arkansas; Corydon, Indiana; Dexter, Missouri; and Noel, Missouri. The company plans to shift production to other facilities and estimates total charges of $300 million to $400 million. This comes as part of Tyson's efforts to cut costs and achieve $1 billion in productivity savings by the end of fiscal 2024. Tyson also reported a loss of $417 million in its fiscal third-quarter financial results.
