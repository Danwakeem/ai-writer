## Fulton County DA to have most of staff work remotely for 3 weeks
Fulton County District Attorney Fani Willis plans to have most of her staff work remotely for three weeks in August, and is asking judges not to schedule trials and in-person hearings during part of that time. This comes as her office continues to scrutinize actions by former President Trump and others as they tried to overturn his loss in Georgia. Willis also notes the potential for significant public reaction to the announcement of charging decisions.

## One killed and one injured in targeted shooting at Ohio engine manufacturing facility
A male suspect carried out a targeted attack at an Ohio engine manufacturing facility on Thursday evening, killing one employee and injuring another who is being treated for non-life-threatening injuries. The suspect suffered a self-inflicted gunshot wound and is also being treated for injuries. The motive is unknown and the facility, which produces turbo-diesel engines for Chevy and GMC trucks, will remain closed overnight.

## Tsunami warning issued for Pacific islands after 7.7 earthquake
A 7.7 magnitude earthquake near the Loyalty Islands prompted a tsunami warning for Pacific islands, including Vanuatu, New Caledonia, and New Zealand. Waves up to 60 centimeters above tide level were measured in some areas, prompting evacuations in Vanuatu. The warning has since passed, but small sea level changes may continue.

## Kenton Cool becomes second Briton to climb Everest 14 times
Kenton Cool, a British mountaineer, has climbed Mount Everest 14 times. He believes that mountains are 'bigger than records' and is pleased with the recent climb. Only Nepali Sherpa guides have scaled the peak more times than Cool. The season is expected to finish at the end of this month, with hundreds of climbers and their local guides planning to reach the summit. Unfortunately, nine people have died on Everest this year.

## Japanese Prime Minister seeks action on nuclear disarmament at G7 summit in Hiroshima
Prime Minister Fumio Kishida has chosen Hiroshima to highlight nuclear nonproliferation efforts, but critics doubt his disarmament goals. An estimated 12,705 nuclear warheads are in inventory, with most held by the U.S. and Russia. During the G7 summit, Kishida will seek support for his Hiroshima Action Plan, calling for non-use of nuclear weapons, transparency, and a nuclear stockpile reduction. Survivors of the atomic bombings in Hiroshima and Nagasaki hope that younger generations will carry on their unfinished work.
